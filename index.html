Bio

I am a machine learning researcher in the Center for Big Data at Tsinghua University. Meanwhile, I am working on machine learning research advised by professor Qiang Liu at UT Austin. My goal is to develop data-efficient and trustworthy machine learning systems that can continuously improve. Towards this end, my current research focuses on Trustworthy Machine Learning and Reinforcement Learning. More specifically, my latest efforts explore a number of topics including (i) learning monotonic neural network, (ii) developing interpretable machine learning methods, and (iii) improving off-policy evaluation in reinforcement learning.

Prior to joining Tsinghua, I was a graduate student at Harvard advised by professors Jeffrey Frankel and Carmen Reinhart. I conducted research at the intersection of finance and machine learning. Before that, my study and work at MIT lied in the broad area of decesion-making under uncertainty advised by professor Richard de Neufville.
